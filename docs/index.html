Computational Physics · Quantum Materials

Exploring emergent behaviour in low-dimensional systems.

I lead the Quantum Complexity Lab at Example University, where we investigate how topology and disorder shape the transport properties of novel materials. My work bridges analytical theory, large-scale simulation, and collaborations with experimental groups around the world.

Research Themes

Current directions

We combine analytical techniques with high-performance computing to understand emergent phenomena at the nanoscale. These focus areas highlight ongoing projects and opportunities for collaboration.

Topological transport

Revealing the role of Berry curvature in charge transport across engineered heterostructures.

Machine-learned Hamiltonians

Building surrogate models that accelerate discovery of material phases under extreme conditions.

Quantum thermalization

Probing how disorder and interactions govern relaxation in isolated quantum systems.
